Coffee Museum


A cultural historical heritage

The construction of the official coffee exchange palace, a work by Companhia Construtora de Santos, in an eclectic style, is considered the most important work of the period. Style, volume, copper domes, large sculptures, stained glass, marble, the work of foreign craftsmen and works of art built the discourse that linked the coffee elite to the first bandeirantes as pioneer builders of a nation led by São Paulo. Thus, in 1920, Companhia Construtora de Santos began work on the building of the Official Coffee Exchange. The construction portrays a mixture of several architectural movements, especially the neoclassical and baroque styles. For the façade, Companhia Construtora de Santos purchased pink granite from Salto de Itu, designed by the Belgian architect Ernest Chaineux and executed by the stonemasons Antônio and José Longobardi. Right at the entrance we come across a small stained glass window above the doors with the symbol “United States of Brazil”, the name of our country on the date of opening of the building and the coat of arms of Brazil, composed of two branches: one of coffee and the other of smoke . The clock tower is about 40 meters high and at its top four sculptures stand out in architecture, representing agriculture, commerce, industry and the sailor.


ARCHITECTURE AND ARTWORKS


In architecture, the neoclassical and baroque styles stand out.


In the Auction Hall is the greatest concentration of works of art and architecture characteristic of the building. In this room, negotiations for the purchase and sale of grain were held, and there are several Masonic symbols, such as the six-pointed star in the center of the floor and the organization of the stalls and columns. The furniture is arranged in an octagonal shape and represents the hierarchy of the Exchange: the board of directors with the president in the center and brokers around it. The salon also has a set of works designed and executed by the painterBenedicto Calixto, considered an icon of Santos art: a stained glass window and three paintings, as follows:


Foundation of Villa de Santos — 1545

Técnica: Óleo sobre tela

Date: 1921-1922

Dimensions: It is a triptych (three canvases that form a scene) measuring 3.25m high by 9m wide


In the canvas "FOUNDATION OF VILA DE SANTOS - 1545" the author presents the São Paulo family as the genesis of Brazilian civilization by establishing the degrees of kinship, succession and hierarchy in political, military and religious circles of the characters that make up the scene. This location currently corresponds to the region of Praça da República. Further ahead, the author begins to describe the buildings on the panel, starting with the Igreja da Misericórdia. It should be noted that in addition to the Church, the building housed the hospital that gave rise to the Santa Casa de Misericórdia de Santos.


In the work "PORT OF SANTOS IN 1822", we see the Jail and Conjunto do Carmo with the churches of the first and third order and the convent. From there you can see the Itororó stream, which has its source on Monte Serrat and flows into the port. Then, closer to the hill, we see Campo da Misericórdia with the church and Hospital da Santa Casa. Going a little further to the right towards the hill, we find Nossa Senhora do Rosário Church. Returning to the port area we see the Jesus Maria José church and advancing a little after the São Bento creek we can see the Conjunto do Valongo, administered by the Franciscans, with the church of Santo Antônio and the convent. In the frame of this panel we can see four medallions arranged at the ends. In the upper left corner we see a coat of arms with the inscription: Brasil Colônia 1549–1816. In the upper right corner we can read: Brasil-Kingdom 1816–1822. In the lower left corner — Work and Order, illustrated by a hive of bees. Bottom right — Farming and Commerce, illustrated by a winged helmet and the caduceus of Mercury, God of Commerce.

Title: Port of Santos in 1822

Technique: Oil on canvas

Date: 1921-1922

Dimensions: 325x247 (AxL)cm


Title: Port of Santos in 1922

Technique: Oil on canvas

Date: 1921-1922

Dimensions: 325x247 (AxL)cm

In "O PORTO DE SANTOS EM 1922" the artist shows all the transformations triggered by the export of coffee: this new city is the political reflection of immigration and urban development. Calixto chose a viewpoint from Pacheco Hill. There we can see the port channel that separates the islands of São Vicente and Santo Amaro forming an “L” upside down. In the frame of this panel we can see four medallions received at the ends. At the top left, it reads: Brazil Empire — 1822–1889. Top right: Brasil Republica—1889–1922. At the bottom left: Arts and Industry, whose illustration exposes a set of objects: a cogwheel, a painting palette, a crafted capital, a lyre recalling music and poetry, the bust of Carlos Gomes and the first page from the score of “O Guarani”. At the bottom, on the right: Evolution and Progress, highlighting the positivist conception of the artist.


Title: The Epic of the Bandeirantes

Technique: Stained Glass Created in the "Art Nouveau" Style

Date: 1921-1922


The stained glass window “A EPOPEIA DOS BANDEIRANTES” is divided into three parts, which refer to different historical moments in Brazil. In the center “The vision of Anhanguera, the Mãe D’Ouro and the Mães D’água”, representing the colonial period and the conquest of the sertões by the bandeirantes; on the sides “Agriculture and Abundance”, from the imperial period, and “Indústria e Commercio” from the republican period. In addition, it is composed of eight busts of bandeirantes and a frame decorated with agricultural genres, fauna, flora, and beings from Brazilian folklore. Despite seeking an interpretation of national history from the São Paulo perspective, Calixto chose a fantastic approach, full of allegories, mixing aspects of a classic European mythology with mythological elements, Brazilian fauna and flora. This composition, added to the tropical luminosity provided by the chosen glass colors, denote an intention to attribute a Brazilian quality to the work, even if in a conservative manner.

INFOGRAPHIC


1920 - START OF WORKS

The Exchange began operating in 1917 in a small office, and at the end of the 19th century, the development of the coffee economy triggered a broad process of urban transformations in the city of Santos. This period, characterized by some authors as the Belle Epoque of Santos, began the need to build a monument that symbolized this economic peak.

1922 - INAUGURATION

The Palácio da Bolsa Oficial de Café is inaugurated, with two theories emerging for the choice of this date, the first in honor of the opulence and strength of the São Paulo coffee elite and the second in honor of the centenary of the proclamation of independence Benedito Calixto is in charge of executing the screens and decorative stained glass for the trading floor, where negotiations on the price of the product would take place. The inauguration of the Bolsa Oficial do Café palace with works by Benedito Calixto took place on September 7, 1922.

1970 - BUILDING ABANDONED

With the end of Bolsa do Café activities, the building was abandoned, its maintenance being relegated to a few works for small structural conservation, which were not always sufficient to solve the problems presented.

1986 - CLOSING OF THE COFFEE EXCHANGE

The Governor of the State of São Paulo decrees the official closure of the Santos Coffee Exchange.

1996 - CREATED THE COFFEE MUSEUM PROJECT

The Governors of the State of São Paulo met with public and private bodies to create the "COFFEE MUSEUM" project, and in 1997, work began to restore and save the Palácio da Bolsa do Café building.

1998 - INTEGRATION OF THE MUSEUM TO THE BOLSA DO CAFÉ PALACE

After the restoration and reopening of the Official Coffee Exchange Palace, it was integrated with the newly created COFFEE MUSEUM.

2000 - INAUGURATION OF THE MUSEUM'S COFFE SHOP

The Museum Cafeteria is inaugurated, offering a varied menu and, for its excellence in preparation, it is awarded the "PREMIUM" status by the Brazilian Association of the Coffee Industry (ABIC).

2009 - LISTENING OF THE BUILDING BY IPHAN

The construction of the Palace, due to its eclectic style, is considered the most important work of the period and, therefore, it was the first building in this style to be listed by the National Historical and Artistic Heritage Institute (IPHAN).

2015-2016 - RESTORATION OF THE STAINED GLASS "THE EPIC OF THE BANDEIRANTES"

Between 2015 and 2016, the stained glass window "THE EPIC OF THE BANDEIRANTES" underwent a careful restoration process, which took place in a temporary exhibition called "DISCONTRUINTING AN EPIC" that represented a small scenographic atelier with mock-ups of tools used in the manufacture and restoration of stained glass windows.

AT THE MOMENT

Nowadays, the Coffee Museum receives more than 350,000 visitors a year, who take advantage of the visit to the museum to discover other tourist attractions in the Historic Center of Santos in a true journey through time, which can be done on foot.
